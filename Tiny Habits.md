## 6. How to Make a Habit Easier to Do

To make habits easier, **reduce friction and simplify the action**. This includes:

* Breaking habits into tiny steps.
* Optimizing your environment so cues are obvious.
* Designing habits that take less effort — the easier it is to start, the more likely you are to repeat the behavior.



## 7. How to Make a Habit Harder to Do

To break unwanted habits, **increase friction and remove cues** that trigger the habit. This means:

- Making the unwanted behavior less convenient or less visible.
- Deleting distracting apps or placing barriers between you and the habit.
- Eliminating triggers from your environment entirely.



## 8. A Habit I Want to Do More Of Daily Coding Practice

- **Make the cue obvious** - Keep my laptop ready on my desk and set a fixed time every day for coding.
- **Make it attractive** - Choose interesting problems or work on projects that excite me.
- **Make it easy** - Start with just 20–30 minutes instead of long sessions.
- **Make it satisfying** - Track progress daily and reward myself after completing practice.



## 9. A Habit I Want to Eliminate Excessive Phone Usage

- **Make the cue invisible** - Keep my phone away from my study area.
- **Make it unattractive** - Remind myself how it wastes time and affects productivity.
- **Make it hard** - Uninstall distracting apps or turn off notifications.
- **Make it unsatisfying** - Set limits and track wasted time to stay aware of usage.
